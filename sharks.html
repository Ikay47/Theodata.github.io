Shark Attack report

Introduction

This report presents the findings of an extensive data analysis conducted on shark attacks from 1900 to 2022. The dataset, consisting of approximately 5000 entries, was obtained from the quantum analytics google drive.

Objective

The primary objective of this analysis was to perform an exploratory data analysis to identify trends and gain insights into shark attacks. Additionally, the aim was to provide recommendations to mitigate and minimize the occurrence of attacks and fatalities.

Methodology

The dataset was imported into Power BI and underwent thorough data transformation using the power query editor. The dataset required significant cleaning, particularly in the species column, which involved employing functions such as trim, clean, and replace values.
Firstly, columns with null values were filtered out, and incorrect species names were replaced with the appropriate ones. Subsequently, any incident records dated before 1900 or after 2022 were removed from the dataset.

Key Insights

  1. Metrics The analysis revealed the involvement of 47 different shark species, with the white shark being the most commonly reported attacker.
  2. Sex Distribution Approximately 89% of the victims were males, which aligns with the well-known tendency of men to engage in more adventurous activities. The median age of the victims was determined to be 24 years.
  3. Fatalities The report indicates a fatality rate of approximately 6%. However, due to the lack of available information, the severity and consequences of the injuries could not be accurately ascertained.
  4. Incident Types Around 4000 attacks were classified as unprovoked, while 536 were categorized as provoked. Notably, there were almost 2000 attacks where the incident type was undocumented or unknown.
  5. Time Analysis The majority of incidents occurred during the middle of the year, with additional spikes in January and December. These periods often coincide with holidays or vacation periods. It is worth noting that the highest reported incidence was recorded in 1905, followed by a significant decline. This drop may be attributed to underreporting. However, incident rates gradually increased from 1929 to 2016 before experiencing another decline. This gradual rise can be attributed to population growth.
  6. Location The United States had the highest number of recorded attacks, followed by Australia, which accounted for approximately 50% of their respective records.

Recommendations

  1. Increase the dissemination of education and information on safety measures and emergency management, with a particular focus on young males between the ages of 18 and 30.
  2. Conduct comprehensive surveys of vacation sites to identify potential dangers or the presence of shark populations. Implement proactive measures to prevent disasters, including the presence of shark and safety experts at tourist destinations.
  3. Ensure clear and meticulous documentation of incident data collection to avoid ambiguity during future analyses.

In conclusion, this data analysis provides valuable insights into shark attacks from 1900 to 2022. The findings and recommendations can inform efforts to minimize the occurrence of shark attacks and enhance public safety. Continuous data collection and analysis will be crucial in maintaining an up-to-date understanding of this complex phenomenon.
